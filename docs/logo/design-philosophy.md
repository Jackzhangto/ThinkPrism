# ThinkPrism Logo — Design Philosophy

**Movement name:** Prism Mind

---

## Philosophy

**Light as thought.** A prism does not invent colour; it reveals what is already in the light. The logo embodies the same idea: one clear shape that splits and redirects attention into multiple modes of thinking. The form should feel like a single beam entering, then diverging into distinct rays—each ray precise, intentional, and traceable back to the source. No decorative excess; every line serves the metaphor of decomposition and clarity.

**Space and form.** The mark lives in generous negative space. The prism element is geometric and minimal: a triangle or diamond that suggests refraction without literally depicting a physical prism. Where the rays meet or converge, they form a second gesture—a soft curve or bubble that suggests a mind or thought. This convergence is the “Think” in ThinkPrism: many ways of thinking focused into one coherent output. The composition is balanced so the icon reads at small sizes (favicon, extension icon) and holds up at large sizes (splash, store asset).

**Color and material.** Palette is strict and intentional. Deep blue `#1E3A8A` carries authority and depth—the “one beam” or the stable base. Cyan `#22D3EE` is the refracted light: the accent that suggests clarity, focus, and the moment of insight. Gradients between blue and violet-blue are allowed where they reinforce the prism effect; no stray colours. The result should feel like a tech product with a single, memorable hue pair—not a rainbow, but a controlled spectrum.

**Scale and rhythm.** The logo is a single unit: one symbol that can sit alone (icon) or beside the wordmark “ThinkPrism”. Rhythm comes from the rays—either a small number (three to five) or a continuous fan—creating a sense of direction and flow toward the focal point (mind/bubble). Repetition is minimal; the power is in simplicity and one clear idea.

**Composition and balance.** The prism/ray element and the mind/bubble element share the same visual weight. Neither overwhelms the other. If the mark is above the wordmark, the wordmark is set in a clean, modern sans-serif (Inter or Manrope), thin to medium weight, with ample letter-spacing. All elements sit within safe margins; the lockup is suitable for square (icon) and horizontal (full logo) formats.

**Visual hierarchy.** In the icon form, the prism-and-rays read first; the mind/bubble reads as the consequence. In the full logo, the symbol may sit to the left of “ThinkPrism” or above it; the wordmark is legible and secondary to the symbol. The result is unmistakably professional—the product of meticulous craft and restraint, as if every curve and angle had been refined for hours to achieve a single, confident statement.

*This philosophy guides the visual expression of the ThinkPrism logo: one prism, one spectrum, one mind. Output as a single PNG suitable for extension icon and store assets.*
